The cavalry academy

It was installed in Valladolid in 1852 in an octagonal building of 1847 that was intended to be used as a prison. In 1915 there was a fire and between 1921-27 the current building was constructed to the plans of Adolfo Pierrad.

National Sculpture museum

The collection is exposed in the St. Gregory College itself, a beautiful example of fifteenth century architecture, famous for the richness and originality of its decoration, as well as its importance in Spanish history.

The Church of Saint Pau

The ornamentation is dedicated, in good part, to the exaltation of the Dominican order. It also includes numerous little angels, animals, plant motifs and the fleur de lis, emblem of the founder and confessor of Isabel la Católica.

The Campo Grande Park

Right in the city centre lies its most emblematic park in terms of both history and its beauty as a garden.The park occupies a surface area of 115,000 m2 and combines in a unique way its plant life with artistic, aquatic, children’s playground and environmentally friendly features.
